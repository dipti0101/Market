N-Methyldiethanolamine (MDEA) is an organic chemical compound with the formula C5H13NO2. It is a colorless to pale yellow liquid that is both a tertiary amine and a diol. MDEA is primarily used as a gas-treating agent, particularly in removing hydrogen sulfide (HâS) and carbon dioxide (COâ) from natural gas and refinery process streams. Due to its selective absorption properties, it is favored in situations where COâ needs to be removed without significantly removing hydrocarbons.
