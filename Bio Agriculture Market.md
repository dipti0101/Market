Bio Agriculture, also known as Biological Agriculture, refers to farming practices that emphasize the use of natural processes and biological products to improve soil fertility, crop protection, and sustainability. It avoids synthetic chemicals like fertilizers, pesticides, and herbicides, instead promoting organic materials, biodiversity, and ecological balance. The goal is to create a healthy farming ecosystem that supports long-term productivity while minimizing environmental impact.
