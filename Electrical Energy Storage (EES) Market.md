United States Electrical Energy Storage (EES) Market Research Report 2024-2030
The United States Electrical Energy Storage (EES) market size was valued at US$ 8.76 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach US$ 18.92 billion by 2030, at a CAGR of 13.7% during the forecast period 2024-2030.

Electrical Energy Storage (EES) systems are technologies designed to store electrical energy for later use, including batteries, pumped hydro, compressed air, and other emerging storage solutions, crucial for grid stability and renewable energy integration.

The US Electrical Energy Storage market is experiencing rapid growth, driven by the increasing penetration of renewable energy, grid modernization efforts, and the need for resilient power systems. In 2023, total installed capacity reached 32.5 GWh, with lithium-ion batteries accounting for 70% of market value. The market saw a 40% increase in utility-scale battery storage installations in 2023, driven by falling costs and supportive policies. Use of flow batteries for long-duration storage is growing at 35% annually. The market is witnessing a trend towards hybrid storage systems, with a 50% year-over-year increase in projects combining batteries with other storage technologies. Adoption of behind-the-meter storage in commercial and industrial applications grew by 45% in 2023. Leading developers are focusing on advanced energy management systems, with a 60% increase in deployments of AI-powered optimization software for storage assets. The industry is also seeing increased interest in vehicle-to-grid (V2G) technologies, with a 70% rise in pilot programs exploring electric vehicles as distributed storage resources.
