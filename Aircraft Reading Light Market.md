An aircraft reading light is a small, adjustable light fixture located above each passenger seat, typically in the overhead panel. It allows passengers to illuminate their personal space without disturbing others, making it easier to read or perform tasks in low-light or dark cabin conditions. These lights are designed to be directional and can be turned on or off by the passenger.
